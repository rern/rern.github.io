DTC Troubleshooting: P0976 (8-3)

DTC P0976:  Short in Shift Solenoid Valve B Circuit


NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.

1.Clear the DTC with the HDS.

2.Start the engine in the P position, and keep the shift lever in the P for more than 1 second.

3.Check that DTC P0976 recurs.

Is DTC P0976 indicated?


YES - Go to 7.


NO - Go to 4.


4.Choose Shift Solenoid B in Miscellaneous Test Menu, and test the shift solenoid valve B with the HDS.

Is the system normal?


YES - Go to 5.


NO - Go to 7.


5.Start the engine in the P position, and keep the shift lever in the P for more than 1 second.

6.Monitor the OBD status for P0976 in the DTCs/Freeze Data in A/T Mode Menu for a pass/fail.

Does the result indicate FAILED?


YES - Go to 7.


NO - Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check the SH B (GRN/WHT) wire for an intermittent short to ground between shift solenoid valve B and the PCM. If the tester indicates NOT COMPLETED, return to 4 and recheck.n

7.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

8.Jump the SCS line with the HDS.

9.Disconnect PCM connectors B (44P) and C (44P).

10.Measure resistance between PCM connector terminals B26 and C40 or C44.

 

Is there less than 12 W?


YES - Go to 11.


NO - Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM , then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original  PCM.n

11.Disconnect the shift solenoid harness connector.

12.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminals B26 and C40 or C44.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Repair short to ground in the wire between PCM connector terminal B26 and the shift solenoid harness connector, then go to 14.


NO - Go to 13.


13.Inspect shift solenoid valve B.

Is shift solenoid valve B OK?


YES - Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original  PCM.n


NO - Replace shift solenoid valve B or shift solenoid harness, then go to 14.

14.Clear the DTC with the HDS.

15.Start the engine in the P position, and keep the shift lever in the P for more than 1 second.

16.Monitor the OBD status for P0976 in the DTCs/Freeze Data in A/T Mode Menu for a pass/fail.

Does the result indicate PASSED?


YES - Troubleshooting is complete.n


NO - Return to 7 and recheck.
