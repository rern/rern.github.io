A/T Interlock System - Shift Lock System Circuit Troubleshooting

1.Connect the HDS to the DLC.

2.Choose Shift Lock Solenoid Test in Miscellaneous Test Menu, and check that the shift lock solenoid operates with the HDS.

Does the shift lock solenoid work properly?


YES - Go to 14.


NO - Go to 3.


3.Remove the center console.

4.Disconnect the shift lock solenoid connector.

5.Turn the ignition switch ON (II).

6.Measure voltage between shift lock solenoid connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.

 

Is there battery voltage?


YES - Go to 7.


NO - Check for a blown No. 10 (7.5 A) fuse in the under-dash fuse/relay box. If the fuse is OK, repair open or short in the wire between the shift lock solenoid connector and the under-dash fuse/relay box.n

7.Shift the shift lever into the P position, and press the brake pedal. Do not press the accelerator.

8.Measure voltage between shift lock solenoid connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2 while pressing the brake pedal.

 

Is there battery voltage?


YES - Go to 9.


NO - Go to 10.

9.Release the brake pedal, and measure voltage between connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2. The shift lever must be in the P position.

 

Is there battery voltage?


YES - Repair short to ground in the wire between PCM connector terminal A27 and the shift lock solenoid.n


NO - Check the shift lock mechanism. If the mechanism is OK, replace the shift lock solenoid.n

10.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

11.Jump the SCS line with the HDS.

12.Disconnect PCM connector A (44P).

13.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal A27 and shift lock solenoid connector terminal No. 2.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Substitute a known-good PCM and recheck.n


NO - Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal A27 and shift lock solenoid connector.n

14.Press the brake pedal.

Are the brake lights ON?


YES - Go to 14.


NO - Repair faulty brake light circuit.n


15.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

16.Jump the SCS line with the HDS.

17.Disconnect PCM connector A (44P).

18.Measure voltage between PCM connector terminal A40 and body ground while pressing the brake pedal and when the brake pedal is released.

 

Is there battery voltage while the brake pedal is pressing, and no voltage when the pedal is released?


YES - Go to 20.


NO - Go to 19.

19.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal A40 and brake pedal position switch 4P connector terminal No. 2.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Substitute a known-good PCM and recheck.n


NO - Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal A40 and the brake pedal position switch.n

20.Connect PCM connector A (44P).

21.Disconnect the transmission range switch connector.

22.Turn the ignition switch ON (II).

23.Measure voltage between transmission range switch connector terminals No. 6 and No. 10.

 

Is there about 5 V?


YES - Go to 28.


NO - Go to 24.

24.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

25.Disconnect PCM connector B (44P).

26.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal B13 and body ground.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Repair short in the wire between PCM connector terminal B13 and the transmission range switch connector.n


NO - Go to 27.


27.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal B13 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 6.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Go to 28.


NO - Repair open in the wire between PCM connector terminal B13 and the transmission range switch connector.n

28.Check for continuity between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 10 and body ground.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Substitute a known-good PCM and recheck.n


NO - Repair open in the wire between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 10 and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101).n

29.Test the transmission range switch.

Is the switch OK?


YES - Go to 29.


NO - Replace the transmission range switch.


30.Connect the HDS to the DLC.

31.Check the APP SENSOR in the data list with the HDS. Do not press the accelerator.

Is the accelerator pedal position sensor opening 11 % and above, or the sensor voltage 0.90 V and above?


YES - Check the APP sensor.n


NO - Substitute a known-good PCM and recheck.n
