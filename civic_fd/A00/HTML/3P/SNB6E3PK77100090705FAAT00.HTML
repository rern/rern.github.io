DTC Troubleshooting: P0705 (5-2)

DTC P0705:  Short in Transmission Range Switch Circuit (Multiple Shift-position Input)


NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.

1.Clear the DTC with the HDS.

2.Start the engine.

3.With the brake pedal pressing, move the shift lever through all positions. Stop for at least 1 second in each position, and monitor the OBD status for P0705 in the DTCs/Freeze Data in A/T Mode Menu for a pass/fail.

Does the result indicate FAILED?


YES - Go to 4.


NO - Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for an intermittent short in the wire between the transmission range switch and the PCM. If the tester indicates NOT COMPLETED, return to 2 and recheck.n


4.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

5.Inspect the transmission range switch.

Is the switch OK?


YES - With the switch connector disconnected, go to 6.


NO - Replace the transmission range switch, then go to 48.

6.Turn the ignition switch ON (II).

7.Measure voltage between transmission range switch connector terminals No. 6 and No. 10.

 

Is there battery voltage?


YES - Go to 13.


NO - Go to 8.

8.Turn the ignition switch OFF.

9.Jump the SCS line with the HDS.

10.Disconnect PCM connector B (44P).

11.Check for continuity between PCM connector terminal B13 and body ground.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Repair short in the wire between PCM connector terminal B13 and transmission range switch, then go to 48.


NO - Go to 12.

12.Check for continuity between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 10 and body ground.

 

Is there continuity?


YES - Update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original  PCM.n


NO - Repair open in the wire between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 10 and ground (G101), or repair poor ground (G101), then go to 48.
